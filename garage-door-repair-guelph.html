Garage Door Repair Costs & What to Expect

Most garage door repairs in Guelph fall into a few common categories, and knowing the typical cost range helps you spot a fair quote. Spring replacement is the most frequent call — torsion springs run $180–$350 and extension springs run $120–$220, always replaced as a pair since the second spring is usually close to failure once the first breaks.

Opener repair typically runs $150–$300 depending on whether the issue is a sensor, remote, drive gear, or motor. Cable and roller replacement runs $120–$250, and is worth addressing quickly — a snapped cable under tension is a genuine safety hazard, not just an inconvenience. Track and panel repair runs $150–$400 depending on the extent of the damage; a single bent section of track or one damaged panel can often be repaired or swapped without replacing the whole door.

Same-day and emergency service is available seven days a week for doors that are unsafe to leave as-is — stuck open, off track, or with a snapped spring or cable. We diagnose the actual cause of the failure rather than just replacing the most obvious part, since components like springs, cables, and rollers wear together and a single fix without addressing the underlying issue often fails again quickly.

New Door or Repair? How to Tell

An isolated failure — a broken spring, a faulty opener, a bent track section, or worn cables and rollers — is almost always a repair, not a replacement. Full replacement starts to make more sense when the door itself is over 15–20 years old, the panels are rusted or structurally cracked, it's poorly insulated for an attached garage, or you're seeing repeated breakdowns across multiple systems within a short period.

If you're weighing the two, we're happy to give you a straight answer either way — see our garage door installation services if a new door turns out to make more sense.
