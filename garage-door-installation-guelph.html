Planning a New Garage Door in Guelph

A new garage door is one of the highest-return home upgrades in Guelph — it's the largest single visual element on most home exteriors, and a tired or damaged door drags down curb appeal fast. It's also a functional upgrade: modern doors seal better, insulate better, and pair with openers that are quieter and smarter than what was available even five or ten years ago.

Choosing a door type. Steel doors remain the standard choice for Guelph homes — durable, affordable, and available in dozens of colour and window-panel combinations. Carriage-style doors cost more but deliver a distinctive, higher-end look while running on the same standard track and spring hardware as a steel door, so long-term maintenance and repair costs are comparable.

Sizing and installation. Standard single doors run roughly 8–9 feet wide; standard doubles run 16 feet. Older Guelph homes, especially in the downtown and university-area neighbourhoods, sometimes have non-standard openings that need a custom-sized door — we measure your exact opening before quoting so there are no surprises.

Typical cost ranges. A standard single steel door installed runs $900–$1,600; a standard double runs $1,600–$2,800. Insulation typically adds $200–$500. Carriage-style and custom doors run higher depending on materials, window inserts, and hardware finish. Every quote is itemized so you know exactly what you're paying for.

What's included. Every installation includes an in-home measurement and consultation, supply of the door and hardware, professional installation, removal and disposal of your old door, and a walkthrough of your new opener's features. Most installs are completed in a single visit.

Not Sure If You Need a New Door or a Repair?

Not every garage door problem calls for a full replacement. If your door is generally sound but has a broken spring, a stuck opener, a bent track, or damaged rollers or cables, a repair is usually faster and more cost-effective. Replacement makes the most sense when the door is over 15–20 years old, the panels are rusted or cracked, it's poorly insulated, or you're dealing with repeated breakdowns across multiple components.

If you're not sure which situation applies to you, we're happy to take a look and give you an honest recommendation either way — see our garage door repair services for spring, opener, cable, and track repairs in Guelph.
